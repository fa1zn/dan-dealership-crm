# Ride-along field notes

The instrument for watching one real rep work their territory for a half-day (ride-along) or two
hours (screen-share). Print it, or keep it open on a second device. The event stream
(`docs/telemetry.md`) captures the quantitative half; this captures what a log can't: why they did
it, what they said, what their face did.

## Rules for the observer

- Do not help. Do not explain. Do not answer questions about the app unless the rep is about to
  bounce, then answer the minimum and go back to watching.
- If they misunderstand something, that's the truth, not their mistake. Write what they thought,
  not what it "was supposed to" do.
- Timestamp everything. Note the screen they were on.

## Mode-specific setup

**Ride-along:** sit in the passenger seat. Bring a second device for this sheet so you're not
crowding their phone. Do not narrate. Let silences sit.

**Screen-share:** ask them to narrate what they're trying to do before each action ("I'm about
to..."), share their actual phone screen (not a desktop mirror if the real usage is mobile), and
work their real territory, not a sandbox. 90 minutes minimum, 2 hours better.

## Writeup (after — one page, not a report)

- What I saw (3-5 bullets, concrete).
- What surprised me (the thing that wasn't in any of our assumptions).
- Top 3 things to fix before the next ride-along, ranked.
- The one to fix this week.
